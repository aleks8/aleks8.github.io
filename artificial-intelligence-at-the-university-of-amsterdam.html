Artificial Intelligence at the University of Amsterdam

Motivation Essay
Visualize physically disabled preschoolers dancing on stunning emerald fields with breathtaking views, hopeful architecture, bountiful sedentary activities and inspiring nature paths. This will be the beginning of dementia “Maggie Centers”, building on research by AnnMarie Adams, with computer vision and AI assisted architecture. As well as indoor design, art and VR therapies with curated images and scenes for captivating calm, or adventure, to be fully accessible to all. Or, after a dementia diagnosis in a tired, frazzled, and hopeless state, families will drop off their aging parent returning a while later to find a dignified person, with a much healthier body, mind, and spirit.
A few talented researchers at U of Amsterdam: [1] Dr. Belleman, Bosse and Bredeweg, in building VR therapy re-education systems that have a keen understanding of stress, communication and motivated learning, [2] Dr. Endriss, to change laws reflecting current cultural assumptions on aging because of his focus on social change research, [3] Prof Gevers, with his strides in the computer analysis of human behavior, can help to analyze the healing aspects, as well as optimal upgrades to VR therapy, [4] To pull a wide range of diverse art into the VR machines, Prof Worring and Gjorgji Strezoski’s work on OmniArt, [5] Dr. Hoogendoorn’s work on machine learning and health to thoroughly analyze hospital data, [6] Dr. Klein’s behavior change support research due to dementia’s connection to depression and anxiety, [7] Karen Ullrich’s work on deep learning for music, a very promising area of therapy, [8] Prof. Welling’s research on algorithms for “billion data-items” as designing hospitals takes into account a large number of technical components. Amsterdam has created an environment of tolerance, compassionate healthcare, new art, and improving policies.
These ideas started in my third year of Computer Science studies at McGill University when my 54 year old mother was diagnosed with early onset dementia. My father, brother and I care for her by paying close attention to what works and collecting interesting unsolved problems towards a greater goal of full health. This has required personal awareness, exploration, self-directedness and creativity. Through this experience I know I would excel at U of A and get a step closer to creating humane, technological, interdisciplinary advances in the delivery of healthcare Hanna systems.
