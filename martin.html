Martin and the Cultivation of Other


From time immemorial men [sic] have lived by the principle that ‘self-preservation is the first law of life.’ But this is a false assumption. I would say that other-preservation is the first law of life. It is the first law of life precisely because we cannot preserve self without being concerned about preserving other selves. The universe is so structured that things go awry if men [sic] are not diligent in their cultivation of the other-regarding dimension. (King, 1967, p. 80). Note: Last name not mentioned in the blog link because it felt like a strangely watered down cliche to post a positive blog entry titled MLK. Although, I am aware that it may be read as disrespectful; therefore, I want it to be clear that is not my intention. Even though the road to hell was paved with good intentions I hope it is collectively received as a fresh and renewed view of his lesson and wisdom.
